# Program-Operator Business Case

Veil Scout is aimed first at ecosystem program operators: hackathon organizers, grant programs, incubators, launchpads, and developer ecosystem teams.

## Buyer and Job to Be Done

The buyer needs to identify credible builders, justify follow-up support, and keep post-event execution visible after the initial ranking moment. Today this usually happens through spreadsheets, manual judge notes, Discord messages, and ad hoc grant reviews.

Veil Scout packages that workflow as one auditable loop: AI Prior, human correction through Crowd Odds, transparent settlement, scout reputation, and milestone-based incubation.

## Alternatives

- **Manual review:** flexible, but slow and hard to audit after the event.
- **Grant voting:** good for community participation, but often weak at evidence quality and follow-through.
- **Generic prediction markets:** produce odds, but usually lack builder-specific evidence, non-transferable scout reputation, and post-event incubation.
- **AI-only scoring:** fast, but brittle if reviewers cannot inspect provenance, challenge assumptions, or compare with human conviction.

## Differentiation

Veil Scout is not just a prediction UI. Its wedge is the combination of:

1. AI Prior with provider, model, prompt version, input digest, and evidence digest;
2. human scout correction through non-transferable credits;
3. on-chain settlement and persistent scout reputation;
4. separate milestone incubation with authorized, evidence-led release actions.

That combination makes it easier for a program operator to answer both questions: “Who looked promising?” and “Who kept executing?”

## Pricing Hypothesis

No revenue is claimed as live. The initial monetization hypothesis is:

- program license per hackathon, grant round, or incubator cohort;
- verification usage for AI evidence generation and milestone checks;
- optional operator analytics for builder retention and scout accuracy.

## 90-Day Pilot Metrics

A credible pilot should track:

- builder retention after the event;
- verified milestones completed;
- reviewer time saved versus manual review;
- scout accuracy after settlement;
- number of builders moved from initial discovery into follow-up support.

## Current Evidence Boundary

There are no customer interviews or product-market fit claims in this submission. The current evidence is a working technical workflow, test coverage, and a public-proof-ready demo path. Market validation remains a next milestone.
